StratOS

Strategic Intelligence Operating System

"Tell the system who you are, and it tells you what matters today."

The system improves its own scorer through a distillation loop — Claude Opus re-scores articles, disagreements become training data, and LoRA fine-tuning updates the local model.

Self-Improving Scorer

Two-tier feedback loop: implicit user signals (save/dismiss/rate) plus Claude Opus distillation at ~$0.40/cycle. Disagreements become training data.

DoRA fine-tuning permanently updates local model weights. V2 was trained on 20,550 examples across 30 profiles spanning 20 countries. The scorer achieves 98.1% direction accuracy with 0.393 MAE.

Profile-Aware Scoring

Same article, different score depending on who's reading. A K-pop manager gets 8.5 on idol news; a pipeline welder in Edmonton gets 0.0.

Context hash (role + context + location) uniquely identifies each intelligence context. SHA-256 normalized, 12-char hex prefix. Retention system filters signals by active profile.

Evolution
Training Journey

19 failed training runs. Each one taught something the documentation never mentioned. The path from a flat scorer to 98.1% accuracy wasn't straight.

V1 · Runs 15–18

Answer-Only Training — The Flat Scorer

5,679 examples with Sonnet distillation. Answer-only format (SCORE: X | REASON: Y) without reasoning traces. Model memorized patterns, gave everything 8.0–8.5. Profile awareness spread: 1.04 — essentially random.

~$17 · Failed
Investigation

Diagnosis: Imitation vs. Reasoning

Research confirmed the issue: Orca showed imitation models learn style, not reasoning. Only explanation tuning transfers capability. The model needed to see the "thinking" process, not just the answer.

Key insight
AMD ROCm

AOTRITON NaN Gradients

TORCH_ROCM_AOTRITON_ENABLE_EXPERIMENTAL=1 causes NaN during backward pass with DoRA + gradient checkpointing + bf16 on 7900 XTX. Days of debugging. Solution: keep disabled for training.

Hardware pain
V2 Design · Run 19

Chain-of-Thought with Native Think Blocks

Switched to Opus Batch API for teacher scoring. 20,550 examples across 30 profiles in 20 countries. Qwen3's native <think> blocks enabled natural CoT training. DeepSeek-R1 showed distillation into 8B could beat GPT-4o.

~$52 · Breakthrough
V2 · Production

98.1% Direction Accuracy

10.7 hours training on 7900 XTX. MAE dropped from 1.553 to 0.393 (75% reduction). Profile awareness jumped from 1.04 to 7.90 (7.6×). Zero parse failures across 2,048 eval examples. Deployed to production.

About
Who I Am

I'm a Computer Engineering student at the American University of Kuwait, graduating Spring 2027. StratOS started because I was tired of opening a dozen tabs every morning — jobs, news, stocks, tech — just to feel caught up. I wanted one place to be "in the know" on everything I care about, ranked by how much it actually matters to me. So I built it.

That "one place" somehow turned into 53,000+ lines of code with a self-improving AI scorer, multi-user authentication, a distillation pipeline that costs $0.40 per improvement cycle, and 19 failed training runs that each taught me something the documentation never mentioned. I focus on ML pipelines, full-stack systems with local-first principles, and getting GPU training to work on AMD hardware — which, if you know ROCm, you know is its own adventure.

I'm looking for systems and ML engineering roles in Kuwait's energy sector, where AI meets industrial operations and the problems are actually interesting.
